Differences between zipcodes

On average there are more interventions in neighborhoods with more rat sightings. A linear regression fit that models the data best yields that on average a given zipcode
with additional 100 rat sightings per year, has around 200 additional interventions per year. However some zipcodes are underserved with interventions. The following plots
show how the number of rat interventions compares to the number of rat sightings in each NYC zipcode. A couple Bronx zipcodes particularly stand out (shades of blue) as
well as a couple of Brooklyn zipcodes (dark pink) - while these zipcodes have comparable numbers of rat sightings, some Bronx zip codes receive up to 6 times more inspections.

about NYC Rats Project

Rats are a large problem in New York City. They present a health threat, can contaminate food, spread diseases and reduce quality of life. They can also damage utilities and contribute to the erosion of buildingsâ€™ structural integrity. In the last years the rat population in the city increased as New Yorkers logged more than 17,200 sightings in 2016, up from 12,600 in 2014. In an attempt to reduce the number of NYC rats mayor Bill de Blasio just last year declared to dedicate $32 million to fight the most problematic rat areas in the city.

'NYC Rats' is the capstone project I am focusing on as a fellow at The Data Incubator. In the project I am exploring rat sightings across New York City reported via the 311 system. I have a dataset that contains reported rat sightings since 2010 until today (in total around 110 000 logged events) and a dataset that contains rodent interventions/inspections in the city, also since 2010 until today (in total around 1.4 million logged events). This totals to around half of a gigabytes of data. Both datasets (among other things) contain locations of a sighting or intervention, time of event and type.

I am making predictions using time series forecasting with Facebook Prophet.
